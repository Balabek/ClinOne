Rob Bohacs

Co-Founder & Chief Solutions Officer

Denver, CO

Connect with me

Rob Bohacs is the Founder and Chief Solutions Officer of ClinOne, who works with world-class pharmaceutical companies to rethink and redevelop how clinical trials should be managed, with patient centricity at its core. Rob believes that it’s time to completely alter how we manage research site and patient interaction, and bring trials directly to patients to simplify a very complex process through technology and automation.

Rob has spent the last 10 years within clinical research, including as CEO of an oncology CRO and site management organization that unified 320 oncology research centers across the U.S.Rob has received several accolades, including several ‘Top 50 Companies to Watch 2016’, a ‘Top 19 Companies to Watch’ award in 2019, ‘Top Clinical Technology’ in 2019, the cover of CIO Applications Magazine in 2018 and cover and feature story of Pharma Tech Outlook Magazine in 2019. Rob holds a Master’s in Business Administration from Graziadio School of Business and Management, Pepperdine University, Los Angeles.

Luke Embree

CTO

Philadelphia, PA

Connect with me

Luke is a technology innovator and leader with 25 years of experience in identifying business goals with executive teams, developing innovative technology strategies, and delivering high-quality solutions. Luke’s vast expertise includes cloud, databases, system performance, scalability, system integration, and data analytics.

As CTO, Luke expands and scales ClinOne technology to support the strong, planned growth of the platform as well as long-term corporate objectives. He oversees a dynamic roadmap to add new features and products to meet and anticipate customer needs, with a focus on rapid, high-quality innovation.

Previously Luke was VP of Engineering at Signant Health, where he directed software development for several products and solutions including eCOA, IRT/RTSM, patient engagement, and clinical supply chain management. Luke also was VP of Technology at prescription medication discount provider FamilyWize, where he led all aspects of technology. Also previously, Luke was Director of Core Development at eCommerce retailer Pet360, where he oversaw infrastructure, DevOps, ERP, business intelligence, supply chain, and systems integration. For 12+ years, Luke was Director of Production Support & Data Services at Citi Prepaid Services, where he helped support growth from $2 million to $200 million.

Luke earned his Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science from Shippensburg University of Pennsylvania. He also took courses toward a Master’s degree in Technology Management at Villanova University.

Dr. Elizabeth Esterl, RN

VP, Medical Affairs & Research

Denver, CO

Connect with me

Beth Esterl is VP of Medical Affairs and Research at ClinOne. Her unique qualifications, based on a strong background in both health care administration and graduate education focusing on nursing leadership, provides her with an effective complement of broad-based technical, medical, and administrative knowledge, combined with the depth of regulatory understanding, for the business of health care.

Beth’s diverse experience includes development and implementation of two successful clinical trials organizations, management of multi-site clinical study projects, personnel and financial management, and coordinating team efforts to provide clinical care and regulatory oversight, protocol development and implementation, IND/IDE applications, and IRB submissions for cutting-edge research and clinical care. Throughout her career, Beth has been the primary principal investigator for clinical research projects within oncology, immunology, allergy, and pediatric specialties as well as sub-investigator to many more pediatric specialties. Beth is also adjunct faculty for Regis University for RN to B.S.N. students and M.S. nursing students.

Beth has been fortunate to teach a variety of classes including but not limited to: nursing roles, nursing theory, nursing research, curriculum development, teaching and learning, community practicum and community nursing, and capstone. In addition, Beth is a visiting professor at the University Medical and Pharmacy School in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam where she teaches Nursing Research for M.S. nursing students.
